Pando.

One tree. Every machine.

Your code shouldn't live on a laptop. Pando keeps your working tree — uncommitted edits, branches, secrets and all — alive on every machine you sit down at.

The name

A forest that is secretly one tree.

In Utah there's an aspen grove called Pando — Latin for “I spread.” It looks like a forest of thousands of separate trees. It isn't. Every trunk grows from a single shared root system: one organism, wearing many trunks.

Your machines are the trunks. Your working tree is the organism. Pando is the root system underneath.

The problem

Git protects what you've published. Nothing protects the rest.

Every lost afternoon of work ever lost lived in the same place: the gap between your keyboard and your last push. Dirty files, untracked experiments, local branches, .env secrets — trapped on whichever machine you happened to be sitting at.

The stranded edit

“It's on the other laptop.”

Mid-refactor, four dirty files, and the machine that has them is closed in a bag across town. Your evening is hostage to hardware.

The wip commit

“WIP — do not review”

You pollute your history with snapshot commits just to move your own work between your own machines. Git as a courier service it never wanted to be.

The spilled chai

One dead laptop, one lost week

Everything committed survives on GitHub. Everything else — branches you never pushed, the day's work, your secrets — dies with the disk.

The vision

Machines become viewports. The work lives in the fabric.

Close the lid mid-thought on one machine, open another, and it is exactly that moment, resumed — same dirty files, same branches, same stash, same secrets, same seeded database. Not because you synced. Because there was never anything to sync to. There is one working tree, and every machine renders it.

Mon · 11:47pm · macbook

Four modified files, two untracked experiments, a half-written commit message. You close the lid. No commit, no push, no ritual.

Tue · 9:04am · linuxbox

cd repo — and it's all there. The dirty tree, the stash, the branch, the thought. You resume the sentence mid-word.

An agent grinds through a migration in its own leased branch. Friday over coffee, you review its actual working tree from the macbook — no push, no PR dance.

A laptop dies. You feel nothing — there was never anything on it. Sign into any machine and the grove regrows the trunk.

Git is your history.
Pando is your presence.

How it works

Proven parts, composed with taste.

Nothing here is new computer science — it's git's object model, Dropbox's watcher discipline, and pnpm's content-addressed stores, arranged around one idea: only ever hold the gap between keyboard and last push.

01 · snapshots

A shadow ring-tree of your work

A quiet daemon snapshots your whole working tree every few idle seconds into a content-addressed DAG — like growth rings. Whole-repo time travel over uncommitted work: “show me Tuesday, 4:03pm.”

02 · overlays

GitHub carries the base. We carry the gap.

Everything pushed already lives on GitHub's CDN. A snapshot is just base commit + overlay — your dirty files, unpushed branches, stash and secrets. Kilobytes, not gigabytes. Pushing shrinks your overlay toward zero.

03 · leases

Machines take turns, like you do

You have one body. Sitting down at a machine acquires the repo's write lease; everyone else reads. No corruption, no conflict-copy litter — and when life diverges offline, an explicit fork-and-merge, never silent loss.

04 · rehydration

Derived state is rebuilt, never copied

Theo was right: node_modules and venvs can't travel across systems. So Pando syncs the recipe (the lockfile) and rebuilds per-platform from a warm, content-addressed artifact cache — before you've opened your editor.

source → synced secrets → encrypted derived → rebuilt local junk → ignored

Local-first, always

Every machine is a first-class trunk with real files on real disk. Planes, cafés, dead wifi — you keep working; the roots catch up.

End-to-end encrypted

The fabric stores ciphertext. We cryptographically cannot read your working tree — a promise the platforms will never match, because they want to.

Git stays sacred

Pando lives under git, never inside it. Your commits, your workflow, your team's process — untouched. We move state; git moves meaning.

Remote-agnostic

GitHub, GitLab, a bare remote on your own box — the base layer is wherever your history lives. The roots don't care.
